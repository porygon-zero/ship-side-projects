# Product Discovery

Use discovery to reduce uncertainty about the problem, users, demand, usability, feasibility, or outcome—not to justify a predetermined solution.

1. Identify the target user or stakeholder segment, current behavior, unmet need, and available evidence. Separate the underlying need from the requested solution, and separate observation from assumption and proposal.
   When the builder is the primary user, firsthand experience is valid problem evidence but not evidence of broader demand. State explicitly whether the project is for personal utility, learning, a community, open-source contributors, or prospective customers.
2. State the riskiest hypothesis and its confidence. Distinguish problem validation from solution validation.
3. Choose the cheapest ethical method that can change the decision: interviews, observation, prototype test, data analysis, concierge test, technical spike, or controlled experiment.
4. Define success and invalidation thresholds, guardrails, evidence limitations, and the decision each result would trigger before collecting data.
5. Minimize personal data. Obtain authority for research, telemetry, recruitment, incentives, or production experiments when required.
6. Prefer behavioral outcome signals over output counts. Use leading indicators for early learning and lagging indicators for durable value; record known confounders.
7. After the experiment, record the result as supported, weakened, invalidated, or inconclusive. Update, reorder, or remove backlog items accordingly; do not preserve a hypothesis merely because implementation has begun.

For a personal tool, a repeated real workflow and observable time, quality, or enjoyment improvement may be sufficient validation. For a product intended for others, seek evidence from representative users before scaling scope or infrastructure. Do not turn a side project into a compulsory startup: the builder may reasonably optimize for learning, craft, or enjoyment when that outcome is explicit.

Use `assets/templates/experiment.md` when a durable experiment record is justified.
